Overview​

Introduction

Entities in a biological system seldom work in isolation. There is a coordinated association between them at every level of the molecular hierarchy. These associations are context-specific and involve making and breaking of links among the entities depending upon the developmental stage, tissue and environmental conditions. In field conditions, plants are generally exposed to multiple stresses. Thus finding processes that are common or unique across various stress conditions can provide novel insight towards developing multi-stress tolerant crops. In NetREx, user can explore coordinated association between genes under different abiotic stress conditions and two phyto hormone treatments.

Graph Construction

A typical transcriptomic experiment (microarray or RNA-seq) detects thousands of genes differentially expressed with respect to control conditions. A functional analysis of such a large set of genes is a daunting task. A popular approach is to construct co-expression networks based on the similarity of gene expression profiles. Here, nodes represent genes and edges represent the strength of correlations between them. Highly correlated genes can then be grouped into functional modules, reducing the dimensionality of data. Another approach is to convert raw correlation values (Pearson or Spearman) to highest reciprocal ranks (HRRs) or mutual ranks (MR). Several studies have shown that the biological relevance of ranked-based methods are more significant and robust than those constructed using raw PCCs because they intuitively consider PCCs of the neighbourhood of gene pairs as well.

Dataset

For the construction of this resource environmental stresses (e.g., drought, cold, flood, salinity) and plant hormone treatments (abscisic acid (ABA) and jasmonic acid (JA)) have been used. All the experiments have been performed using a single mRNA-Seq platform under standardized laboratory conditions and from the same rice cultivar Oryza sativa L. (cv. Nipponbare), making it possible to compare gene expressions across different stress conditions. In NetREx, data for four stress conditions (drought, cold, flood and osmotic stress) and two hormonal treatments (ABA and JA) from seedlings (10 days after germination) have been considered for network construction and analysis. Two global rank-based stress networks across four stress conditions and two hormonal treatments, have been constructed for root and shoot tissues. Stress-specific networks are then derived from these global networks using differentially expressed genes.

Viewers

Network Viewer

It displays connectivity between query genes for a given stress condition and tissue type. Nodes are coloured coded based on their expression levels (up/down w.r.t. normal), module membership, and sized and coloured based on their degree. Based on the functional annotation, biological pathways and processes that are activated or repressed under given stress condition can be identified. Comparing it across two or more stress conditions, processes that are unique or common to the processes can also be identified.


Network Neighbourhood Viewer

It displays top neighbours of query genes, identified based on their connectivity in the complete network. This can help in better capturing biological pathways and processes that are activated or repressed under given stress condition can be identified.


Module Viewer

Genes that are co-expressed are put together in the same module. Thus by careful analysis of query genes that are part of the same module, biological processes and pathways that are activated/repressed and co-regulated can be identified. In this view, top 100 highly connected genes based on their within-module connectivity (HRR100) is shown and the genes are displayed with colour of the modules they belong to. The “Module Attribute” table provides the GO enriched terms for biological processes.
